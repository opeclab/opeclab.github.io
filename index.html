Welcome to OPEC Lab at Australian National University & University of Otago.

Lab Mission

We aim to use silicon photonics to overcome the limitations of current computing architectures, which are constrained by the slowing performance of traditional semiconductors and the shift towards data-intensive, learning-based paradigms, while addressing challenges in power consumption, efficiency, and reliability in highly parallel systems.

Future photonic accelerators

Designing reconfigurable heterogeneous photonic accelerator architectures using emerging photonic computing technologies.

Emerging interconnect technologies

Emerging interconnect technologies (photonic, wireless, RF, hybrid) for Network-on-Chips (NoCs) and embedded systems.

Advanced parallel computing models

Future parallel computing models and architectures including Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Deep Neural Networks (DNNs), near-data computing, and approximate computing.

Cloud computing and edge computing

Design a heterogeneous model combining cloud computing and edge computing to meet the needs of applications such as autonomous driving, real-time video processing, and intelligent manufacturing.
